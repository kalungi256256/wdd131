Welcome to Uganda

Uganda, known as the "Pearl of Africa," is a landlocked country in East Africa renowned for its incredible biodiversity, stunning landscapes, and warm, welcoming people. From the snow-capped Rwenzori Mountains to the vast savannas of Queen Elizabeth National Park, Uganda offers some of Africa's most spectacular wildlife experiences.

Home to more than half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas, Uganda provides unparalleled opportunities for wildlife enthusiasts. The country is also the source of the mighty River Nile, offering adventure sports, cultural experiences, and breathtaking natural beauty that captivates visitors from around the globe.

Top Attractions

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park

Home to approximately half of the world's mountain gorillas, this UNESCO World Heritage site offers unforgettable gorilla trekking experiences through ancient rainforest.

Source of the Nile

Witness where the mighty River Nile begins its 6,650-kilometer journey to the Mediterranean Sea at Jinja, offering white-water rafting and scenic boat cruises.

Queen Elizabeth National Park

Uganda's most popular savanna park, famous for tree-climbing lions, boat safaris on the Kazinga Channel, and diverse wildlife including elephants, hippos, and crocodiles.

Rwenzori Mountains

The legendary "Mountains of the Moon" offer challenging treks through unique alpine vegetation zones, glacial lakes, and snow-capped peaks reaching over 5,000 meters.

Lake Victoria

Africa's largest lake provides fishing experiences, island hopping adventures, and beautiful sunset views. The Ssese Islands offer pristine beaches and relaxation.

Murchison Falls National Park

Witness the dramatic Murchison Falls where the Nile forces through a narrow gorge, and enjoy game drives featuring the Big Five animals in Uganda's largest national park.
